## MODULE 10: IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE

### Week 1-2: Foundation

- Share evidence synthesis with team
- Create pocket cards or quick reference guides
- Alert stakeholders (ICU director, pharmacy, nursing)
- Define baseline metrics

### Week 3-4: Education & Training

- Deliver presentation (use presentation templates)
- Train nursing and allied health staff
- Create EHR order sets or documentation templates
- Pilot protocol on target unit

### Month 2-3: Rollout & Monitoring

- Full protocol implementation
- Weekly compliance tracking
- Collect adverse events and feedback
- Adjust protocol based on real-world experience

### Month 3+: Evaluation & Sustainment

- Analyze outcomes vs. baseline
- Gather multidisciplinary feedback
- Publish QI data (consider manuscript)
- Embed in annual curriculum
- Update protocol as new evidence emerges

## MODULE 11: BIBLIOGRAPHY STANDARDS

### Format

```
[#] Author(s). Title. Journal. Year;Vol(Issue):Pages. PMID: xxxxxxx. DOI: xx.xxxx/xxxxx
```

### Organization

Organize by evidence tier:

1. **Tier 1A** — Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
2. **Tier 1B** — High-quality RCTs
3. **Tier 2** — Cohort studies, lower-quality RCTs
4. **Tier 3** — Case-control studies, case series
5. **Tier 4** — Expert opinion, consensus statements
6. **Guidelines** — Society guidelines (ATLS, EAST, WEST, ACCP, SCCM)

### Requirements

- Every claim in the synthesis must have a citation
- All PMIDs must be verified against PubMed
- Never fabricate study data, effect sizes, or citations
- If data is limited, state: "Current high-quality data for this exact metric are limited"
- Aim for 20-30 references (standard synthesis) or 50-75 (comprehensive package)
