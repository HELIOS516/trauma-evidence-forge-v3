## Permissive Hypotension Reduces Mortality in Penetrating Trauma

- Target MAP 50-60 mmHg until surgical hemorrhage control<sup>[1]</sup>
- Bickell trial: 70% vs 62% survival favoring delayed resuscitation<sup>[2]</sup>
- Contraindicated in TBI (maintain MAP >80 mmHg)<sup>[3]</sup>

> **Bottom Line:** Permissive hypotension improves survival in penetrating trauma without TBI.

**Sources:**

- [1] Bickell WH et al. N Engl J Med. 1994;331(17):1105-1109. PMID: 7935634
- [2] Dutton RP et al. J Trauma. 2002;52(6):1141-1146. PMID: 12045644
- [3] Carney N et al. Neurosurgery. 2017;80(1):6-15. PMID: 27654000

## 1:1:1 Transfusion Ratio Improves 24-Hour Survival

| Outcome             | 1:1:1 Group | 1:1:2 Group | p-value |
| ------------------- | ----------- | ----------- | ------- |
| 24-hr mortality     | 9.2%        | 14.6%       | 0.03    |
| Hemostasis achieved | 86%         | 78%         | 0.006   |
| 30-day mortality    | 22.4%       | 26.1%       | 0.12    |

- PROPPR trial: 680 patients, 12 Level I trauma centers<sup>[1]</sup>
- Earlier hemostasis with balanced ratios<sup>[1]</sup>

> **Bottom Line:** 1:1:1 plasma:platelet:RBC ratio achieves faster hemostasis and reduces early mortality.

**Sources:**

- [1] Holcomb JB et al. JAMA. 2015;313(5):471-482. PMID: 25647203

## TXA Within 3 Hours Reduces Hemorrhage-Related Death

- CRASH-2: 20,211 patients, 1.5% absolute mortality reduction<sup>[1]</sup>
- Benefit limited to administration within 3 hours<sup>[1]</sup>
- No increase in vascular occlusive events<sup>[1]</sup>

> **Bottom Line:** Administer TXA 1g IV within 3 hours of injury for all trauma patients with significant hemorrhage.

**Sources:**

- [1] CRASH-2 collaborators. Lancet. 2010;376(9734):23-32. PMID: 20554319

## Viscoelastic Assays Guide Targeted Transfusion Therapy

| Parameter       | TEG    | ROTEM | Clinical Meaning         |
| --------------- | ------ | ----- | ------------------------ |
| Clot initiation | R-time | CT    | Clotting factor activity |
| Clot formation  | K-time | CFT   | Fibrinogen function      |
| Clot strength   | MA     | MCF   | Platelet function        |
| Fibrinolysis    | LY30   | ML    | Clot stability           |

- TEG/ROTEM reduces blood product use by 20-30%<sup>[1]</sup>
- Enables personalized coagulation management<sup>[2]</sup>

> **Bottom Line:** Viscoelastic assays reduce unnecessary transfusion and improve goal-directed therapy.

**Sources:**

- [1] Gonzalez E et al. Ann Surg. 2016;263(6):1051-1059. PMID: 26720428
- [2] Baksaas-Aasen K et al. Intensive Care Med. 2021;47(1):49-59. PMID: 33048195
