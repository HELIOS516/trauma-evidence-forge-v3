# Emergency General Surgery Evidence Guide

Comprehensive reference for EGS condition categories, guideline societies, severity grading systems, and evidence synthesis frameworks.

## EGS Condition Categories

### Acute Biliary Disease

**Conditions:** Acute cholecystitis, choledocholithiasis, gallstone pancreatitis, cholangitis
**Key Guidelines:** Tokyo Guidelines (TG18/TG13), WSES 2020, SAGES 2010
**Severity Grading:** Tokyo TG18 (Grade I mild, II moderate, III severe), AAST EGS Grade I-V
**Key Decision Points:** Timing of cholecystectomy (early vs delayed), bile duct management, percutaneous drainage indications
**Landmark Trials:** ACDC Trial (early vs delayed LC), CHOCOLATE Trial (LC vs observation for mild)

### Bowel Obstruction

**Conditions:** Adhesive SBO, hernia-related SBO, large bowel obstruction, sigmoid/cecal volvulus
**Key Guidelines:** WSES 2017 (adhesive SBO), Bologna Guidelines, EAST PMG
**Severity Grading:** AAST EGS Grade I-V, simple vs strangulated
**Key Decision Points:** Water-soluble contrast challenge, operative timing (24-72h trial), laparoscopic vs open
**Landmark Studies:** AORC trial (early vs delayed surgery for adhesive SBO)

### Acute Appendicitis

**Conditions:** Uncomplicated, complicated (perforated, abscess), interval appendectomy
**Key Guidelines:** WSES 2020, SAGES 2010, EAST PMG
**Severity Grading:** AAST EGS Grade I-V, Alvarado Score (0-10), RIPASA Score
**Key Decision Points:** Antibiotics-first vs surgery, interval appendectomy after abscess drainage
**Landmark Trials:** CODA Trial (antibiotics vs appendectomy), APPAC Trial

### Perforated Viscus

**Conditions:** Perforated peptic ulcer, diverticular perforation, colonic perforation
**Key Guidelines:** WSES 2020, Hinchey Classification (diverticulitis)
**Severity Grading:** Hinchey I (pericolic abscess), II (pelvic abscess), III (purulent peritonitis), IV (fecal peritonitis)
**Key Decision Points:** Primary repair vs resection, Hartmann vs primary anastomosis, laparoscopic washout (Hinchey III)
**Landmark Trials:** LADIES Trial, SCANDIV Trial (Hartmann vs primary anastomosis)

### Mesenteric Ischemia

**Conditions:** Acute arterial mesenteric ischemia, acute mesenteric venous thrombosis, chronic mesenteric ischemia
**Key Guidelines:** ESVS 2017, WSES 2017
**Key Decision Points:** Endovascular vs open revascularization, second-look laparotomy, anticoagulation
**Landmark Data:** Mortality 60-80% if diagnosis delayed >24h

### GI Hemorrhage

**Conditions:** Upper GI bleed (variceal, non-variceal), lower GI bleed, obscure GI bleed
**Key Guidelines:** ACG 2021 (upper GI bleed), ASGE Standards, International Consensus (lower GI bleed)
**Scoring:** Glasgow-Blatchford Score (upper), Oakland Score (lower), Rockall Score
**Key Decision Points:** Endoscopic timing (<24h, <12h for high-risk), angiographic embolization, surgical indications

### Soft Tissue Infections

**Conditions:** Necrotizing soft tissue infection (NSTI), perianal abscess, complex wound infections
**Key Guidelines:** WSES 2018 (NSTI), IDSA 2014
**Scoring:** LRINEC Score (>=6 suspicious, >=8 strongly predictive): WBC, Hgb, Na, glucose, creatinine, CRP
**Key Decision Points:** Time to OR (NSTI is a surgical emergency), extent of debridement, NPWT, reconstruction timing

### Acute Diverticulitis

**Conditions:** Uncomplicated, complicated (Hinchey I-IV), recurrent
**Key Guidelines:** WSES 2020, ASCRS 2020, AGA 2015
**Severity Grading:** Hinchey Classification, AAST EGS Grade I-V
**Key Decision Points:** Outpatient vs inpatient, antibiotics vs no antibiotics (uncomplicated), percutaneous drainage, elective sigmoidectomy indications

### Hernias (Acute)

**Conditions:** Incarcerated inguinal, incarcerated ventral, strangulated, emergent repair
**Key Guidelines:** EHS 2009/2014, WSES 2019 (emergency hernia)
**Key Decision Points:** Emergent vs urgent repair, mesh vs no mesh in contaminated field, laparoscopic approach

### Acute Pancreatitis

**Conditions:** Mild, moderately severe, severe (Revised Atlanta Classification), infected necrosis
**Key Guidelines:** IAP/APA 2013, ACG 2013, WSES 2019
**Severity Grading:** Revised Atlanta (mild, moderately severe, severe), AAST EGS Grade I-V, Ranson Criteria, BISAP Score, APACHE II
**Key Decision Points:** Fluid resuscitation strategy, early enteral nutrition, step-up approach for infected necrosis, cholecystectomy timing
**Landmark Trials:** PANTER Trial (step-up vs open necrosectomy), PYTHON Trial (EUS vs surgical drainage)

### Acute Care Colorectal

**Conditions:** Malignant colonic obstruction, toxic megacolon, massive lower GI hemorrhage
**Key Guidelines:** WSES 2020, ASCRS
**Key Decision Points:** Stenting vs diverting colostomy vs resection (malignant obstruction), colectomy timing (toxic megacolon), surgical indications for LGIB

## AAST EGS Severity Grading

Anatomic severity grading (Grades I-V) analogous to trauma organ injury scales:

- **Grade I:** Mild, minimal pathology
- **Grade II:** Moderate, localized pathology
- **Grade III:** Severe, regional pathology
- **Grade IV:** Very severe, diffuse pathology or organ compromise
- **Grade V:** Life-threatening, organ failure or perforation with sepsis

Available for: acute cholecystitis, acute appendicitis, acute diverticulitis, small bowel obstruction, acute pancreatitis, colonic obstruction

## Standard EGS Evidence Synthesis Tables

For any EGS condition, produce:

1. Severity classification table (AAST grade + condition-specific system)
2. Operative indications table (absolute vs relative)
3. Timing table (early vs delayed with supporting evidence + GRADE)
4. Guideline comparison matrix (WSES vs SAGES vs EAST vs specialty-specific)
5. Antibiotic recommendations (empiric regimens by condition severity)
6. Outcomes table (mortality, morbidity, LOS by severity and management approach)
