# Global Surgery Key Literature Index

## Foundational Documents

### Lancet Commission on Global Surgery (2015)

- **Citation**: Meara JG, Leather AJM, Hagander L, et al. Global Surgery 2030: evidence and solutions for achieving health, welfare, and economic development. Lancet. 2015;386(9993):569-624. PMID: 25924834.
- **Key contribution**: Defined 6 core indicators, estimated 5 billion people lack access to safe surgery, 143 million additional procedures needed annually.
- **6 Indicators**: (1) Access to timely essential surgery, (2) SAO workforce density, (3) Surgical volume, (4) Perioperative mortality rate, (5) Protection against impoverishing expenditure, (6) Protection against catastrophic expenditure.

### WHO Resolution WHA68.15 (2015)

- **Citation**: World Health Assembly. Strengthening emergency and essential surgical care and anaesthesia as a component of universal health coverage. WHA68.15. May 2015.
- **Key contribution**: First WHO resolution recognizing surgery as essential healthcare component.

### Disease Control Priorities, 3rd Edition - Volume 1 (DCP3, 2015)

- **Citation**: Debas HT, Donkor P, Gawande A, et al, eds. Essential Surgery. Disease Control Priorities. 3rd ed. Vol 1. Washington, DC: World Bank; 2015.
- **Key contribution**: 44 surgical procedures identified as cost-effective; surgery among "best buys" in global health.

## National Surgical Planning

### NSOAP Framework (WHO, 2017)

- **Citation**: World Health Organization. Surgical care systems strengthening: developing national surgical, obstetric and anaesthesia plans. WHO; 2017.
- **Key contribution**: Framework for countries to develop National Surgical, Obstetric, and Anesthesia Plans. Domains: infrastructure, workforce, service delivery, financing, information management, governance.

### Zambia NSOAP (First Published)

- **Citation**: Zambia Ministry of Health. National Surgical, Obstetric, and Anaesthesia Strategic Plan 2017-2021. Lusaka: Ministry of Health; 2017.
- **Key contribution**: First country to publish a complete NSOAP.

## Workforce & Training

### Holmer et al. (2015) - SAO Workforce

- **Citation**: Holmer H, Lanber A, Kunjumen T, et al. Global distribution of surgeons, anaesthesiologists, and obstetricians. Lancet Glob Health. 2015;3 Suppl 2:S9-11. PMID: 25926323.
- **Key contribution**: Mapped global SAO workforce density; target of 20 SAO providers per 100,000 population.

### Chu et al. (2018) - Training Models

- **Citation**: Chu KM, Jayaraman S, Kyamanywa P, Ntakiyiruta G. Building research capacity in Africa: equity and global health collaborations. PLoS Med. 2014;11(3):e1001612. PMID: 24618823.
- **Key contribution**: Framework for equitable North-South research partnerships.

## Surgical Volume & Access

### Rose et al. (2015) - Unmet Need

- **Citation**: Rose J, Weiser TG, Hider P, Wilson L, Gruen RL, Bickler SW. Estimated need for surgery worldwide based on prevalence of diseases: a modelling strategy for the WHO Global Health Estimate. Lancet Glob Health. 2015;3 Suppl 2:S13-20. PMID: 25926315.
- **Key contribution**: Estimated 28-32% of global disease burden is surgically treatable.

### Weiser et al. (2008) - Surgical Volume

- **Citation**: Weiser TG, Regenbogen SE, Thompson KD, et al. An estimation of the global volume of surgery: a modelling strategy based on available data. Lancet. 2008;372(9633):139-44. PMID: 18582931.
- **Key contribution**: Estimated 234 million major surgical procedures annually worldwide; vast majority in HICs.

## Perioperative Safety

### WHO Surgical Safety Checklist

- **Citation**: Haynes AB, Weiser TG, Berry WR, et al. A surgical safety checklist to reduce morbidity and mortality in a global population. N Engl J Med. 2009;360(5):491-9. PMID: 19144931.
- **Key contribution**: Demonstrated 36% reduction in major complications and 47% reduction in mortality with checklist use.

### GlobalSurg Collaborative

- **Citation**: GlobalSurg Collaborative. Mortality of emergency abdominal surgery in high-, middle- and low-income countries. Br J Surg. 2016;103(8):971-88. PMID: 27145169.
- **Key contribution**: Demonstrated 2x mortality in LICs vs HICs for emergency abdominal surgery after adjustment.

## Financial Protection

### Shrime et al. (2015)

- **Citation**: Shrime MG, Dare AJ, Alkire BC, O'Neill K, Meara JG. Catastrophic expenditure to pay for surgery worldwide: a modelling study. Lancet Glob Health. 2015;3 Suppl 2:S38-44. PMID: 25926319.
- **Key contribution**: 33 million individuals face catastrophic expenditure from surgical care annually; 81 million face impoverishing expenditure.

## Trauma & Emergency Surgery in LMICs

### Mock et al. (2012)

- **Citation**: Mock C, Joshipura M, Arreola-Risa C, Quansah R. An estimate of the number of lives that could be saved through improvements in trauma care globally. World J Surg. 2012;36(5):959-63. PMID: 22419411.
- **Key contribution**: Estimated 1.7 million lives could be saved annually by improving trauma care in LMICs.

### Henry et al. (2015)

- **Citation**: Henry JA, Bem C, Grimes C, et al. Essential Surgery: the way forward. World J Surg. 2015;39(4):822-32. PMID: 25566979.
- **Key contribution**: Defined Bellwether procedures (laparotomy, cesarean section, open fracture treatment) as proxies for surgical system capability.

## How to Use This Index

1. **Starting a new paper**: Search this index for relevant foundational citations
2. **Literature review**: Use PMIDs to pull full texts and trace citation networks
3. **Framework selection**: Match your research question to the appropriate framework (Lancet Commission indicators, NSOAP domains, DCP3 procedures)
4. **Evidence gaps**: Note which topics have limited LMIC-specific evidence - these represent research opportunities
