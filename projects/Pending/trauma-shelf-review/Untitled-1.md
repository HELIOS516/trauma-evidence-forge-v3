  - Partial thickness = very painful (most painful of all)

  ## Inhalation Injury Closes the Airway in Hours — Intubate Before It's Too Late

  - Suspect: enclosed fire + singed hairs + carbonaceous sputum
  - CO poisoning: 100% O2 (half-life 250 → 40 min)
  - Cyanide: refractory lactic acidosis → hydroxocobalamin
  - Escharotomy: circumferential burn + absent pulses (NOT fasciotomy)

  **Airway edema progresses over hours — the window closes fast**

  > **Bottom Line:** Enclosed fire + carbonaceous sputum — answer is intubate now, not observe

  **Sources:** [19][20]

  <!-- Speaker Notes:
  If you suspect inhalation injury — enclosed space, facial burns, singed hairs, carbonaceous sputum, hoarse voice — intubate early. Airway
  edema worsens over hours and can make delayed intubation impossible. CO poisoning: give 100% O2 which cuts CO half-life from 250 to 40
  minutes. Pulse ox is unreliable because it can't distinguish carboxyhemoglobin. Cyanide: suspect if lactic acidosis doesn't improve
  despite adequate oxygenation — treat with hydroxocobalamin. Quick note on escharotomy: for circumferential full-thickness burns
  compressing vessels — cut through eschar only, not fascia.

  > Shelf Tip: "Enclosed fire + hoarse voice" = intubate immediately. "Circumferential burn + absent pulses" = escharotomy, not fasciotomy.

  ## Answer: B — 5,120 mL Because the Clock Started at Burn Time, Not Arrival

  - Total 24h: 4 × 80 × 40 = **12,800 mL**
  - First 8h (1400-2200): **6,400 mL**

  - Remaining (1600-2200): 6,400 − 1,280 = **5,120 mL in 6 hours**
