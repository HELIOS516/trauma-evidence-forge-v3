# Post-Op Complications: What Every Student Must Know

## Overview
- **Type:** Periop
- **Duration:** 35 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~13% (post-op complications are reliably high-yield across all surgery shelf exams)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Apply the post-operative fever timeline (Wind/Water/Wound/Walking/Wonder drugs) to narrow the differential by POD
2. Recognize the clinical signs of anastomotic leak and initiate appropriate workup and management
3. Manage VTE prophylaxis failure including both DVT and pulmonary embolism in the post-operative patient

## Key Frameworks
- Wind/Water/Wound/Walking/Wonder drugs: fever differential by post-op day
- Anastomotic leak: POD 3–7, fever + tachycardia + abdominal pain + leukocytosis — CT is diagnostic
- VTE: DVT (wells criteria + duplex) and PE (Wells + d-dimer + CT-PA) in post-op patients
- Clavien-Dindo classification: standardized surgical complication severity grading

## Core Decision Points
- Workup by POD: POD 1–2 atelectasis, POD 3–5 UTI/pneumonia, POD 5–7 wound/anastomosis, POD 7+ DVT
- When to re-image: new fever spike after initial resolution, hemodynamic deterioration, peritonitis development
- When to return to OR: contained leak with IR drainage vs diffuse peritonitis with fecal contamination
