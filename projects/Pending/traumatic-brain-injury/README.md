# Traumatic Brain Injury: GCS to the OR

## Overview
- **Type:** Trauma
- **Duration:** 40 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~12% (TBI is one of the highest-yield trauma topics across shelf exams)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Calculate and interpret the Glasgow Coma Scale at the bedside
2. Identify clinical herniation syndromes and differentiate uncal from central herniation
3. Manage elevated intracranial pressure using the BTF tiered treatment algorithm

## Key Frameworks
- GCS: eyes (1–4), verbal (1–5), motor (1–6) — severe TBI ≤8
- Cushing triad: hypertension, bradycardia, irregular respirations — late sign of herniation
- Brain Trauma Foundation (BTF) 4th Edition guidelines
- Monroe-Kellie doctrine: brain + blood + CSF = constant volume

## Core Decision Points
- When to image: GCS <15 with loss of consciousness, amnesia, or mechanism
- When to evacuate: epidural >1 cm or >30 mL, subdural >10 mm or midline shift >5 mm, symptomatic
- ICP targets: maintain ICP <22, CPP 60–70, avoid hypotension (SBP <90 doubles mortality)
