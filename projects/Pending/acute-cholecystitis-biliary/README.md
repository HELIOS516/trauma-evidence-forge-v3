# Acute Cholecystitis & Biliary Emergencies

## Overview
- **Type:** EGS
- **Duration:** 35 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~12% (biliary disease is the highest-yield elective EGS topic)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Differentiate acute cholecystitis from ascending cholangitis using clinical criteria and the diagnostic triads
2. Apply Tokyo Guidelines TG18 severity grading to determine management intensity
3. Determine the optimal timing of cholecystectomy based on patient condition and disease severity

## Key Frameworks
- Murphy sign: RUQ pain with arrest of inspiration on palpation — 65% sensitivity, 87% specificity
- Charcot triad: fever + jaundice + RUQ pain = cholangitis (not cholecystitis)
- Reynolds pentad: Charcot triad + altered mental status + septic shock = suppurative cholangitis
- Tokyo Guidelines TG18: Grade I/II/III cholecystitis and cholangitis severity scoring

## Core Decision Points
- Early vs interval cholecystectomy: TG18 Grade I/II — early (within 72h) is superior; Grade III — ICU first
- ERCP indications: choledocholithiasis with cholangitis, persistent biliary obstruction, failed conservative management
- Percutaneous cholecystostomy: TG18 Grade III cholecystitis in high-operative-risk patients
