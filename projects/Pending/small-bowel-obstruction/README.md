# Small Bowel Obstruction: When to Watch vs Cut

## Overview
- **Type:** EGS
- **Duration:** 35 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~10% (SBO management algorithm is classic shelf material)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Distinguish partial from complete small bowel obstruction using clinical and radiographic criteria
2. Identify clinical and CT findings indicating strangulation or closed-loop obstruction requiring urgent surgery
3. Apply WSES timing guidelines to determine when to operate and when to continue nonoperative management

## Key Frameworks
- WSES SBO guidelines 2017: partial SBO trial of NOM x 72 hours, complete or strangulation — OR
- Transition zone on CT: site of obstruction, lead point, length of dilated segment
- Strangulation findings: pneumatosis, portal venous gas, mesenteric edema, C-shaped or U-shaped loop
- Water-soluble contrast challenge (Gastrografin): diagnostic and therapeutic for partial SBO

## Core Decision Points
- Conservative vs operative: partial SBO without strangulation signs — 72h NOM; complete or closed-loop — OR within 24h
- Timing of surgery: immediate (strangulation, perforation), urgent <24h (complete, failed NOM), elective (recurrent partial)
- Water-soluble contrast: reach cecum within 24h = 97% chance of resolution without surgery (also accelerates resolution)
