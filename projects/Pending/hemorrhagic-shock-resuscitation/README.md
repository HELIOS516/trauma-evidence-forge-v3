# Hemorrhagic Shock & Resuscitation

## Overview
- **Type:** Trauma
- **Duration:** 40 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~12% (shock and resuscitation are high-yield across all shelf exams)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Classify hemorrhagic shock by class using clinical and physiologic parameters
2. Apply damage control resuscitation principles including balanced blood product ratios
3. Evaluate TXA administration timing and identify patients who will benefit

## Key Frameworks
- Hemorrhagic shock classes I–IV (ATLS)
- Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP): 1:1:1 pRBC:FFP:PLT
- Damage Control Resuscitation (DCR): permissive hypotension + hemostatic resuscitation
- TXA CRASH-2 and CRASH-3 timing windows

## Core Decision Points
- Crystalloid vs blood products: when to abandon crystalloid and transition to balanced resuscitation
- Permissive hypotension: target SBP 80-90 in penetrating, avoid in TBI — when and how to apply
- TXA window: administer within 3 hours of injury; harmful if given after 3 hours (CRASH-2)
