# Thoracic Trauma: Chest Tubes & Thoracotomies

## Overview
- **Type:** Trauma
- **Duration:** 35 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~10% (chest trauma and thoracostomy indications are high-yield)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Diagnose tension pneumothorax clinically and initiate immediate decompression without imaging
2. Apply chest tube output thresholds to identify massive hemothorax requiring operative intervention
3. Identify indications and contraindications for emergency department thoracotomy

## Key Frameworks
- Tension pneumothorax: clinical diagnosis (tracheal deviation, absent breath sounds, hypotension, JVD)
- Hemothorax thresholds: >1500 mL initial or >200 mL/hr x 2–4 hours = operative
- EDT indications: penetrating with witnessed cardiac activity; blunt with <5 min CPR
- Cardiac tamponade: Beck triad (JVD, muffled heart sounds, hypotension)

## Core Decision Points
- Needle vs finger thoracostomy: needle decompression fails in obese/muscular patients — finger is definitive
- Chest tube threshold for OR: initial output >1500 mL or sustained >200 mL/hr x 2–4 hours
- EDT decision: penetrating trauma with recent signs of life has 15% survival; blunt trauma <2%
