# xABCDE: The Trauma Primary Survey

## Overview
- **Type:** Trauma
- **Duration:** 35 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~15% (trauma/critical care emphasis on primary survey)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Apply the xABCDE framework to systematically prioritize life-threatening interventions in a trauma patient
2. Identify clinical and physiologic indications for definitive airway management
3. Classify hemorrhagic shock by class and initiate massive transfusion protocol at appropriate thresholds

## Key Frameworks
- ATLS 11th Edition xABCDE sequence (eXsanguination first)
- "Treat first what kills first" — hemorrhage before airway in penetrating trauma
- MARCH algorithm (Military) vs xABCDE (civilian)

## Core Decision Points
- When to intubate: GCS ≤8, airway obstruction, anticipated deterioration, combativeness limiting resuscitation
- When to needle/finger thoracostomy: clinical tension pneumothorax diagnosis, do not wait for CXR
- When to activate MTP: shock index >1, SBP <90 + mechanism, ABC score ≥2, clinical gestalt
