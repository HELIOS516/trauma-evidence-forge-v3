# Burns: Assessment & Resuscitation

## Overview
- **Type:** Trauma
- **Duration:** 35 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~8% (burns assessment and fluid calculation are reliably tested)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Estimate total body surface area (TBSA) burned using the Rule of 9s and identify partial- vs full-thickness injuries
2. Calculate Parkland formula fluid requirements and adjust based on urine output endpoints
3. Identify burn center transfer criteria per ABA guidelines

## Key Frameworks
- Rule of 9s (adult): head 9%, each arm 9%, chest 18%, back 18%, each leg 18%, perineum 1%
- Lund-Browder chart: more accurate for children and irregular burns
- Parkland formula: 4 mL/kg/% TBSA LR over 24 hours (half in first 8 hours)
- ABA burn center referral criteria (2018)

## Core Decision Points
- Fluid calculation: Parkland is a starting point — titrate to UO 0.5 mL/kg/hr in adults
- Intubation criteria: facial burns + singed nasal hair + hoarseness = intubate before airway swells shut
- Transfer criteria: >10% TBSA partial thickness, any full thickness, hands/feet/face/genitalia, inhalation injury, circumferential
