# Acute Abdomen: A Systematic Approach

## Overview
- **Type:** EGS
- **Duration:** 40 minutes
- **Template:** presentation-medium (18-22 slides)
- **Shelf Exam Weight:** ~15% (acute abdomen DDx is the single highest-yield EGS topic)
- **Target Audience:** MS3/MS4 on ACS/Trauma rotation
- **Presenter:** Evan DeCan, MD — UVA Division of Acute Care Surgery

## Learning Objectives
1. Develop a systematic, anatomy-based differential diagnosis for acute abdominal pain
2. Identify peritonitis and other signs of surgical emergency requiring urgent operative intervention
3. Apply an evidence-based imaging algorithm to acute abdominal presentations

## Key Conditions
- Appendicitis: RLQ pain, Rovsing/psoas/obturator signs, Alvarado score
- Perforated viscus: free air, rigid abdomen, peritonitis — go to OR
- Mesenteric ischemia: pain out of proportion to exam, AF/hypercoagulable state, mortality >50%
- Diverticulitis: Hinchey classification, antibiotic criteria, drainage vs resection
- Small bowel obstruction: closed loop, strangulation signs, operative timing

## Core Decision Points
- CT vs OR: peritonitis on exam goes to OR — imaging delays kill; stable patient gets CT first
- Antibiotics alone vs surgery: uncomplicated diverticulitis managed nonoperatively; complicated requires intervention
- Mesenteric ischemia workup: CTA mesenteric vessels is diagnostic — do not let mild exam reassure you
